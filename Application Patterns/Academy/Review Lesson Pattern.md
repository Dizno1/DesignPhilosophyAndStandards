# Review Lesson Pattern

## Purpose

A review lesson helps learners synthesize a domain, practice professional reasoning, and prepare for assessment.

A review lesson should not behave like a simple quiz dump or a repeated copy of earlier lessons.

## Required role

A review lesson should connect ideas across a domain and help learners recognize how concepts work together in realistic situations.

## Recommended structure

A review lesson may include:

1. Review overview
2. Domain mental model
3. Key concept synthesis
4. Scenario workshop
5. Professional reasoning patterns
6. Common misconceptions and exam traps
7. Connections to earlier domains
8. Exam focus
9. Summary

For capstone review lessons, additional synthesis sections may be appropriate.

## Scenario use

Scenarios should ask learners to reason about accessibility problems, user needs, organizational responsibilities, and professional decisions.

Scenarios should avoid trick questions that depend on obscure wording rather than understanding.

## Exam focus

Exam focus sections should clarify what learners are expected to recognize, not merely list facts.

## Summary

A review lesson summary should close the loop. It should help learners leave with a clear mental model of the domain.

## Why this pattern exists

Review lessons are where scattered knowledge becomes usable professional judgment. The pattern preserves that purpose across Academy courses.
