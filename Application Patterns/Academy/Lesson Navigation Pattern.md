# Lesson Navigation Pattern

## Purpose

Lesson navigation helps learners move through course content without losing their place.

## Required controls

Use only the controls that are meaningful in context.

Common controls include:

- Previous Part
- Next Part
- Related
- Previous Lesson
- Next Lesson
- Home

## Control naming

Button and link text should describe the action.

Avoid relying only on icons or visual placement.

## Disabled controls

Unavailable controls must be communicated clearly.

A disabled control should not be focusable unless there is a specific reason to explain why the action is unavailable.

## Focus behavior

After moving to another lesson part, focus should move to the newly displayed content or to a meaningful heading that identifies the new part.

The learner should not have to hunt for the updated content.

## Screen reader behavior

Status announcements should be concise.

The learner should hear enough to understand what changed without being forced to listen to the whole page again.

## Mobile behavior

Navigation controls should stack cleanly, maintain large touch targets, and remain in a predictable order.

## Why this pattern exists

Learners need movement controls that are predictable, efficient, and respectful of keyboard and screen reader workflows.
