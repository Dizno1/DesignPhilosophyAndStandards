# Academy Design Rationale

## Purpose

This document preserves the reasoning behind the Academy structure.

## Why the interface is predictable

A course becomes easier to use when every lesson behaves the same way. Predictability reduces cognitive load and helps keyboard and screen reader users build efficient navigation habits.

## Why the lesson title matters

The page title and H1 must identify the current lesson because screen reader users often use those cues to confirm location.

A generic H1 such as the course name does not provide enough orientation on lesson pages.

## Why Related Library Topics appear near the top

Related Library Topics are helpful reference material, but they are optional. Placing them near the top makes them discoverable without forcing the learner to read them before continuing.

## Why part controls exist

Long lessons can become difficult to navigate as single uninterrupted pages. Part controls divide lessons into manageable sections while preserving a consistent learning path.

## Why progress is textual

Visual progress bars are not enough. Learners using screen readers, magnification, or high contrast settings need progress information in text.

## Why exam focus and summary come last

In assessment preparation courses, the final instructional rhythm should reinforce what matters for the exam and then close with a summary. This helps the learner leave the lesson with both practical understanding and test readiness.

## Why review lessons are different

Review lessons should synthesize knowledge instead of repeating it. Their purpose is to help learners connect concepts and apply professional judgment.
