# OpenDoorDesign Standards V1

> Version 1.0

This document is the executive overview of the Open Door Design standards. Detailed guidance is contained in the Standards, Patterns, Components, and Philosophy folders.

## Core Principles

- The software works for the user.
- Accessibility is the foundation.
- Compliance is the minimum. Usability is the goal.
- Consistency builds confidence.
- Design for people before technology.

## Living Standard

This repository is the single source of truth for Open Door Design. Standards evolve through real-world development. When a reusable solution is created, it is documented here before being adopted by applications.

## Color and contrast standard

Open Door Design uses WCAG 2.2 AA as the baseline and targets AAA or stronger contrast wherever practical. Normal text should meet 7:1 or better. Primary navigation should target 12:1 or better where practical. The preferred experience is not merely compliant; it should be comfortable, readable, and clear.

Open Door Design projects must not use blue and green together as the primary visual distinction between neighboring interface elements, navigation states, buttons, cards, banners, or important content areas. Passing a contrast checker is required, but it is not enough. Color choices must also be practical for users with common color vision differences.

Green is the primary Open Door Design brand accent. Blue is not part of the primary interface system. Navigation must not use blue links or inherit a blue global link token. Any color-coded meaning must also be communicated through a non-color cue such as text, underline, border, icon shape, spacing, font weight, or label text.
